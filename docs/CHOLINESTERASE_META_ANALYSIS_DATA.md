# Cholinesterase Inhibitors for Alzheimer's Disease: Meta-Analysis Data Compilation

Compiled 2026-07-03 from Cochrane systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and NICE technology appraisals.

---

## 1. Birks 2006 — Cochrane Review (All Three ChEIs Pooled)

**Full citation:** Birks JS. Cholinesterase inhibitors for Alzheimer's disease. *Cochrane Database Syst Rev*. 2006 Jan 25;2006(1):CD005593. doi: 10.1002/14651858.CD005593

**Scope:** 13 placebo-controlled trials, 7,298 patients randomized (2,228 donepezil; 2,267 galantamine; 2,803 rivastigmine), plus 1 head-to-head trial (donepezil vs rivastigmine, 994 patients).

### Pooled Cognitive Outcomes

| Outcome | MD/OR | 95% CI | p-value | N patients | N studies |
|---------|-------|--------|---------|------------|-----------|
| ADAS-Cog (70-pt scale) | MD -2.37 | -2.73 to -2.02 | <0.00001 | 4,236 | 10 |
| MMSE (30-pt scale) | MD +1.37 | 1.13 to 1.61 | <0.00001 | 3,118 | 9 |

- Individual trial ADAS-Cog range: -1.4 to -3.9 points
- Individual trial MMSE range: 0.65 to 2.9 points

### Global Clinical Assessment (CIBIC-Plus)

| Outcome | OR | 95% CI | p-value | N | Studies |
|---------|-----|--------|---------|---|---------|
| Improvement (dichotomized) | 1.56 | 1.32-1.85 | <0.00001 | 3,402 | 8 |
| Improvement or unchanged | 1.84 | 1.47-2.30 | <0.00001 | 1,306 | 2 |

- Absolute rates for improvement: 24% treatment vs 17% placebo

### Activities of Daily Living

| Scale | MD | 95% CI | p-value | N | Studies |
|-------|-----|--------|---------|---|---------|
| DAD | +4.39 | 1.96-6.81 | 0.0004 | 669 | 2 |
| PDS | +2.46 | 1.55-3.37 | <0.00001 | 2,188 | 5 |

### Behavioral Disturbance (NPI)
- MD -2.44 (95% CI -4.12 to -0.76, p=0.004), 1,005 patients, 3 studies

### Estimated NNT (from absolute risk differences)
- NNT for global improvement: ~14 (from 24% vs 17%)
- NNT for improvement or no change: ~7 (from 65.9% vs 51.4%)

### Withdrawals and Adverse Events

| Outcome | ChEI rate | Placebo rate | OR | 95% CI |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-----|--------|
| Total withdrawals | 29% | 18% | 1.76 | 1.54-2.02 |
| Withdrawal due to AE | 18% | 8% | 2.32 | 1.95-2.76 |
| Any adverse event | 72% | 57% | 2.51 | 2.14-2.95 |

### Specific Adverse Events (ChEI vs Placebo)

| Event | ChEI % | Placebo % | OR (95% CI) |
|-------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Nausea | 31.5% | 9.1% | 4.87 (4.13-5.74) |
| Vomiting | 21.4% | 5.4% | 4.82 (3.91-5.94) |
| Diarrhea | 14.4% | 7.9% | 1.91 (1.59-2.30) |
| Dizziness | 14.8% | 7.8% | 1.99 (1.64-2.42) |
| Anorexia | 12.2% | 3.6% | 3.75 (2.89-4.87) |
| Headache | 14.5% | 9.7% | 1.56 (1.27-1.91) |
| Insomnia | 8.5% | 5.9% | 1.49 (1.12-2.00) |
| Abdominal pain | 11.0% | 5.9% | 1.95 (1.46-2.61) |

### Heterogeneity
- Review states "not a high level of heterogeneity as measured by I-squared" but formal I-squared values not reported for most outcomes.
- MMSE showed heterogeneity due to outlier trial RIV-B352 (effect +2.9 vs others 0.65-1.80).
- ADAS-Cog: "Rivastigmine trials show the most variation."

### Head-to-Head: Donepezil vs Rivastigmine (Bullock trial, 994 patients, 104 weeks)
- No significant difference in cognition, ADL, or behavior
- Donepezil better tolerated: withdrawal OR 0.64 (0.50-0.83), AE withdrawal OR 0.47 (0.32-0.68)
- Nausea: 15.2% donepezil vs 32.9% rivastigmine (OR 0.37)
- Vomiting: 5.8% vs 27.9% (OR 0.16)

### Critical Limitation
- The pooled ADAS-Cog effect of -2.7 points is less than the FDA's 4-point minimum threshold for clinical significance.

## 2. Birks & Harvey 2018 — Cochrane Review: Donepezil

**Full citation:** Birks JS, Harvey RJ. Donepezil for dementia due to Alzheimer's disease. *Cochrane Database Syst Rev*. 2018;6:CD001190.pub3.

**Scope:** 30 studies, 8,257 participants. Meta-analyses from 28 studies.

### Dose-Specific Results (26 weeks)

| Outcome | Donepezil 10mg vs Placebo | 95% CI | N | Studies |
|---------|--------------------------|--------|---|---------|
| ADAS-Cog | MD -2.67 | -3.31 to -2.02 | 1,130 | 5 |
| MMSE | MD +1.05 | 0.73 to 1.37 | 1,757 | 7 |
| SIB (severe AD) | MD +5.92 | 4.53 to 7.31 | 1,348 | 5 |
| CGIC (improvement) | OR 1.92 | 1.54 to 2.39 | 1,674 | 6 |
| NPI (behavior) | MD -1.62 | -3.43 to 0.19 | 1,035 | 4 |
| ADL-sev | MD +1.03 | 0.21 to 1.85 | 733 | 3 |

### Donepezil 5mg vs 10mg
- 5mg associated with slightly worse cognitive function on ADAS-Cog (not significant on MMSE or SIB)
- 5mg had slightly better quality of life and fewer adverse events/withdrawals

### Adverse Events (10mg vs placebo)
- Any AE: 72% vs 65%, OR 1.59 (1.31-1.95)
- Withdrawals: 24% vs 20%, OR 1.25 (1.05-1.50)

### From Individual Trial Data (24-week trial)
- Donepezil 5mg ADAS-Cog difference from placebo: 2.49 points (p<0.0001)
- Donepezil 10mg ADAS-Cog difference from placebo: 2.88 points (p<0.0001)
- 4-point improvement rate (observed cases): 53.3% at 10mg vs 26.8% placebo
- 4-point improvement rate (intent-to-treat): 26% at 10mg
- CIBIC improvement: marginal at 5mg (p<0.047), significant at 10mg (p<0.0001)

## 4. Lim et al. 2024 — Cochrane Review: Galantamine

**Full citation:** Lim AW et al. Galantamine for dementia due to Alzheimer's disease and mild cognitive impairment. *Cochrane Database Syst Rev*. 2024. CD001747.pub4.

### Key Results (from search data)
- ADAS-Cog (8-12 mg BID / 16-24 mg/day): MD -2.86 (95% CI -3.29 to -2.43) — high-certainty evidence
- Phase III: 24 and 32 mg/day led to ~3-point treatment effect over placebo
- 32 mg/day was NOT associated with additional cognitive benefit over 24 mg/day

## 6. Blanco-Silvente et al. 2017 — Large Meta-Analysis

**Full citation:** Blanco-Silvente L, Castells X, Saez M, et al. Discontinuation, efficacy, and safety of cholinesterase inhibitors for Alzheimer's disease: a meta-analysis and meta-regression of 43 randomized clinical trials enrolling 16,106 patients. *Int J Neuropsychopharmacol*. 2017;20(7):519-528.

- 43 RCTs, 16,106 patients, 51 drug-placebo comparisons
- Cognitive function SMD: 0.38
- Global symptomatology SMD: 0.28
- Functional capacity SMD: 0.16
- Neuropsychiatric symptoms: not improved
- All-cause discontinuation: OR 1.66
- AE discontinuation: OR 1.75
- Mortality: OR 0.65 (lower with ChEIs)
- Rivastigmine: poorer discontinuation profile
- Donepezil: higher global change efficacy (SMD 0.41)

## 11. Population Impact Measures (Bentham et al. 2008)

**Source:** "What are the benefits of cognitive enhancers for Alzheimer's Disease: use of Population Impact Measures." PMC2206022.

### NNT-Equivalent Data (6 months, treating all eligible mild-moderate AD patients)

**Baseline placebo rates:**
- No deterioration: 57.7%
- 4-point ADAS-Cog improvement: 26.8%
- 7-point ADAS-Cog improvement: 7.8%

**Relative Risk Reduction with treatment:**
- No deterioration: RRR 0.527 (0.321-0.671)
- 4-point improvement: RRR 0.151 (0.01-0.272)
- 7-point improvement: RRR 0.082 (0.004-0.154)

**Per 406 eligible patients treated for 6 months:**
- 123.6 (82.3-169.1) prevented from cognitive deterioration
- 16.4 (2.1-31.2) achieve 4-point ADAS-Cog improvement
- 2.6 (0.2-5.8) achieve 7-point ADAS-Cog improvement

**Implied NNT:**
- NNT to prevent deterioration: ~3.3
- NNT for 4-point (clinically meaningful) improvement: ~25
- NNT for 7-point (strong) improvement: ~156

Treatment adherence at 6 months: 78%.

## 12. Clinical Significance Thresholds

- FDA/EMEA threshold for clinical significance: 4-point ADAS-Cog change
- A 4-point change is roughly equivalent to "a gain of 6 months in terms of delaying decline"
- 40-50% of treated patients show definite improvement (>=4 points ADAS-Cog)
- 20% show strong response (>=7 points ADAS-Cog)
- NNT for significant clinical improvement quoted as 3-7 in some sources (but this varies by definition)
- Natural untreated decline: 9-11 points/year on ADAS-Cog, 2-4 points/year on MMSE
